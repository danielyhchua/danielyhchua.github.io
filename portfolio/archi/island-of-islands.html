Island of Islands

Young Architects Competition, 2016

Poveglia, Venice, Italy

The identity of Poveglia has in recent years been dominated by a romanticised idea of ruin and decay. The abandonment of the island has left the island in an unkempt state of overgrowth, which has often been idealised as nature. In this scheme, we rethink the idea of a natural landscape in the context of an urban environment, rejecting the perception of equating nature with overgrowth.

The superimposition of a North-South urban grid onto the island provides for a new landscape that would mediate between the existing landscape elements, the existing architecture and the new interventions on the island, providing for a homogeneous landscape as a basis for the university.

Each object exerts an influence on the other elements around and by seeing them as islands in the lagoon. We break away from establishing physical and rigid lines of connections and relationships, instead creating zones of radial relationships and diverse spaces of varying uses and privacy. Distinct boundaries are removed from the island, leaving only the physical boundary of the island as a unifying element for the dispersed university volumes and zones on Poveglia.
