Online Architecture Crit

Instructional Design: Harnessing Digital Technologies for Learning, 2020

Monash University, Melbourne, Australia

This is an instructional design project for the Instructional Design: Harnessing Digital Technologies for Learning unit for my Master of Education (Digital Learning) course. The focus of this project is on the rationale behind our instructional design choices. Hence, the images shown here are only a mock-up of the design.

I am designing an online environment that will improve the learning experience for architecture students in a studio-based course. The environment can be viewed on this website. The online environment is intended to be a repository to host architecture students' presentation boards and the video recording of their crit sessions.

By showcasing students' progress on a weekly basis, they will be able to learn from one another's design process, including learning from initial ideas that did not make it to the final design. Having a video recording of crit sessions could also help students refer back to the feedback given by their instructors. The entire online environment could also help instructors during the assessment process as they will be able to view students' portfolio in the online environment.
